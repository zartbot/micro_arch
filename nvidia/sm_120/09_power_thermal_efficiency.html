CHAPTER 09 · SM_120 · GB202 BLACKWELL

Power, Thermal & Efficiency

This chapter presents a comprehensive characterization of the power consumption, thermal behavior, and energy efficiency of the NVIDIA SM_120 (GB202, RTX PRO 5000 72GB) across all supported compute precisions and workload types. Through 17 dedicated experiments (Section 20) and cross-referenced tensor core power measurements (Section 9), we establish that SM_120 operates well below its 350W TDP under all single-workload scenarios, achieves near-perfect DVFS linearity, and exhibits an 864x efficiency span from FP64 CUDA cores (0.011 TFLOPS/W) to Sparse INT8 Tensor Cores (9.49 TOPS/W). The data reveals that Tensor Cores are paradoxically cooler than CUDA cores at comparable or higher throughput, that clock gating is active at per-SM granularity, and that the efficiency-optimal operating point lies at approximately 1500 MHz — 32% more efficient than maximum clock.

9.5.3 SM vs GPC Granularity Discrepancy

Granularity FMA Fit MMA Fit Source
Per-SM scan (Case 12: N ∈ {1,2,4,8,16,32,64,110}) 0.229 W/SM 0.149 W/SM Case 12
14-SM stride scan (Case 14: N ∈ {1,14,28,...,98,110}) 0.743 W/SM 0.115 W/SM Case 14
Discrepancy factor 3.2x ~1x Cases 12, 14

The 3.2× FMA gap reflects different sampling strategies, not contradictory measurements, and is independent of the actual GPC count (which is 11 GPCs × 10 SMs/GPC = 110 SMs per [S3/case_5]):

  • Per-SM scan activates additional SMs within already-powered GPCs across most of its range (e.g., 1→2→4→8 SMs all sit in the same first GPC since one GPC = 10 SMs). The slope (0.229 W/SM) captures only the marginal SM cost; GPC-level shared resources (L1 cache, register-file crossbar, instruction-fetch unit, GPC clock tree) are absorbed into the intercept (~22 W).
  • 14-SM stride scan steps in arbitrary 14-SM increments — these steps are not aligned to the real 10-SM GPC boundary. On average each step crosses ~1.4 fresh GPCs, so the step inevitably powers up new GPC shared infrastructure. The slope (0.743 W/SM) therefore bundles per-SM dynamic cost plus amortized GPC-activation cost, yielding the larger value at R² = 0.977. Dividing 0.743 W/SM by the actual 10-SM GPC size gives ~7.43 W per real GPC (not 10.4 W per 14-SM step × 8 imaginary GPCs).
  • For MMA the two slopes nearly agree (0.149 vs 0.115 W/SM) because tensor-core power saturates at ~30 W delta and is dominated by fixed overhead, leaving little marginal SM cost to redistribute between intercept and slope.

Note on Case 14's stride choice: The 14-SM stride was selected by the test author based on an a-priori estimate of "~14 SMs/GPC". The canonical topology probe [S3/case_5] subsequently confirmed 11 GPCs × 10 SMs/GPC via SM enumeration (nsmid=110), TPC pairing (55 pairs), and atomic-contention bimodal analysis (F=4.55). Case 14's per-step values should therefore be interpreted as "per 14-SM block" (which spans on average 1.4 real GPCs), not as "per GPC".

Combining intercept and slope, the per-SM model predicts full-chip FMA delta ≈ 21.9 + 110 × 0.229 ≈ 47 W, vs Case 12's measured 35.7 W (the ~12 W residual is the sub-linear saturation captured by R² = 0.82). The 14-SM stride model gives 15.5 + 110 × 0.743 ≈ 97 W, vs Case 14's measured 90.3 W at 110 SMs (within ~7 %). Either model confirms that the bulk of the observed ~140 W full-chip power is fixed overhead (L2, memory controllers, I/O, clock tree); only ~25–35 W is the SM-scaled dynamic component.

The DVFS data establishes two actionable deployment strategies: for throughput-maximizing workloads, operate at maximum stable clock (2572 MHz) accepting the 0.37 TFLOPS/W efficiency; for power-constrained deployments (e.g., dense 8-GPU inference racks), operate at 1500 MHz where power drops from ~193W to ~85W (56% reduction) with only 42% throughput loss. The per-SM power model reveals that the bulk of chip power (~105–120W) is fixed overhead (L2, memory controllers, clock tree, I/O ring) regardless of SM count — the compute-dynamic component (FMA: 0.23 W/SM, MMA: 0.15 W/SM) represents only ~25–35W of the total, explaining why partial-SM utilization provides limited power savings.

9.7 Key Insights

KI-9.1: SM_120 Never Reaches TDP Under Any Single Workload (Max 66%)

  • Phenomenon: Maximum observed power is 232W / 350W TDP (66%) after 10 minutes of sustained boost-clock operation ([S20/case_10]). Shorter tests ([S20/case_8], ~24 sec) show only 212W because thermal equilibrium is not reached. No thermal throttle: steady-state 65 degrees C with 27 degrees C headroom below the 92 degrees C threshold.
  • Mechanism: The 350W TDP accommodates multi-GPU NVLink power budget and burst headroom that SM_120 (PCIe-only) cannot fully utilize. Single-workload power is compute-pipeline limited, not TDP-capped.
  • Implication: For inference serving, the 118W headroom (350 - 232) is available for mixed workloads — running TC compute + memory streaming + host transfers simultaneously would still not reach TDP.
  • Confidence: High

KI-9.2: TC Paradoxically Cooler Than CUDA Cores at Comparable TFLOPS

  • Phenomenon: TC MMA FP16 = 108W at 235 TFLOPS. CUDA FP32 FFMA = 143W at 119 TFLOPS (calculated at 2x OPs/FMA). TC delivers 2.18x TFLOPS at 0.76x power.
  • Mechanism: Tensor Cores amortize control/scheduler overhead across 512 FLOPs per instruction (m16n8k16). CUDA cores require per-instruction dispatch for each 2 FLOPs (FMA), expending proportionally more energy on control logic relative to useful compute.
  • Implication: Power-constrained deployments should maximize TC utilization. Even non-standard TC usage (e.g., TF32 matrix operations for non-GEMM workloads) improves energy efficiency by 1.4x over equivalent CUDA core computation.
  • Confidence: Medium

KI-9.3: DVFS Perfectly Linear — Best Efficiency at ~1500 MHz

  • Phenomenon: Throughput scales at 101–102% of ideal linearly from 574 to 2606 MHz. No clock wall observed until ~2610 MHz. Peak efficiency at ~1500 MHz (0.49 TFLOPS/W, 32% better than max clock 0.37 TFLOPS/W).
  • Mechanism: Power scales approximately as V-squared-f while throughput scales as f, so power efficiency monotonically decreases with frequency. No frequency-dependent pipeline hazards exist on SM_120.
  • Implication: For dense 8-GPU inference deployments, operating at 1500 MHz reduces per-GPU power from ~193W to ~85W (a 56% reduction) with only 42% throughput loss. This enables thermal-feasible high-density rack configurations without PCIe thermal limits.
  • Confidence: High

KI-9.4: Power/TOP Halves Per Precision Step — Constant Power, Scaling OPs

  • Phenomenon: All TC formats draw identical ~57W average chip power. TOPS scales with precision: FP16 = 268, FP8 = 536, NVFP4 = 210, Sparse INT8 = 929. TFLOPS/W: FP16 TC = 2.08, NVFP4 OMMA.SF = 2.36, Dense INT8 = 5.04, Sparse INT8 = 9.49 TOPS/W. The per-SM power measurement ([S20/case_16]) shows MXFP4 QMMA.SF = 105 TFLOPS at 89W = 1.18 TFLOPS/W, NVFP4 OMMA.SF = 210 TFLOPS at 89W = 2.36 TFLOPS/W.
  • Mechanism: The TC pipeline executes the same number of MMA instructions regardless of precision. Each instruction processes proportionally more FLOPs at lower precision. Power is dominated by control/scheduling infrastructure, not arithmetic switching energy.
  • Implication: For inference workloads that can tolerate reduced precision, each step down the precision ladder approximately halves power per TOP with zero additional hardware cost.
  • Confidence: High

KI-9.5: Per-SM Clock Gating Confirmed: 0.15 W/SM (MMA), 0.23 W/SM (FMA)

  • Phenomenon: Incremental power per active SM is measurable and distinct per workload type. FMA draws 1.54x more per-SM power than MMA at per-SM scan granularity. Linear scaling (R² = 0.977) for FMA at per-GPC granularity; MMA power saturates at ~30W delta.
  • Mechanism: Inactive SMs are clock-gated (confirmed by [S24/case_31]: zero startup penalty rules out deep power gating). FMA draws more per-SM power because each FFMA instruction activates the full operand collector and writeback path for 2 FLOPs, while HMMA amortizes the same path over 512 FLOPs.
  • Implication: For workloads that cannot fill all 110 SMs, power scales proportionally with active SM count. Recommended budgeting formulas (with intercept included):
  • FMA: P_total ≈ P_idle + 21.9 W + N_active × 0.229 W/SM
  • MMA: P_total ≈ P_idle + 18.7 W + N_active × 0.149 W/SM These per-SM-scan formulas are accurate for N_active ≥ 14 (at least one full GPC active). Below 14 SMs (single GPC partially populated), per-SM cost is dominated by GPC-level fixed overhead and the empirical W/SM curve from Case 12 (5.4–17.1 W/SM at N = 1–16) should be used directly rather than the linear slope.
  • Confidence: High

KI-9.6: The 350W TDP Budget Reveals SM_120's Positioning as a "Compute-Density" GPU

  • Phenomenon: Maximum observed power 232W (66% TDP) under sustained 10-minute workloads ([S20/case_10]). The 118W gap (350 - 232) is never utilized. TC FP16 draws only 108W at 235 TFLOPS. All TC formats draw identical ~57W at chip level.
  • Cross-reference: The per-SM power data (MMA = 0.15 W/SM, FMA = 0.23 W/SM) means 110 SMs x 0.23 W/SM = only 25.3W for full-chip FMA — the remaining power (118–187W) is fixed overhead (L2, memory controllers, I/O, clock tree). This power headroom means SM_120 is thermally "free" for mixed workloads — running TC compute + memory streaming + host transfers simultaneously would still not reach TDP.
  • Implication: For inference serving, SM_120 at 1500 MHz provides 32% better TFLOPS/W (0.49 vs 0.37) with only 42% throughput loss. At 1500 MHz, power drops to ~80W total, enabling dense 8-GPU deployments without PCIe thermal limits. For MoE inference with bursty per-expert compute, the 350W TDP headroom absorbs transient power spikes without throttling.
  • Confidence: High

9.7a Section 24 Follow-Up: Clock Gating and Per-Pipe Power

Section 24 follow-up experiments (Cases 28-31) resolved the clock-gating vs power-gating question and established per-pipe power consumption at SM granularity.

Clock Gating Confirmed (Not Power Gating)

Case 31 measured the startup latency of idle-to-active SM transitions: zero startup penalty was observed. This rules out deep power gating (which requires microsecond-scale power domain ramp-up and state restoration) and confirms that SM_120 uses clock gating for inactive SMs. The clock tree is simply ungated when an SM receives work, providing instant activation.

TC+FMA Co-Issue Power

Case 29 measured power during simultaneous TC and FMA execution: only +0.004 W/SM above TC-only power. This confirms that the FMA pipe's marginal power contribution is negligible when co-issuing with Tensor Cores, consistent with the TC pipeline dominating SM power consumption during mixed workloads.

Power Model: Step Function

Case 28 reveals that SM_120 chip-level power follows a step-function model:

P_total = P_idle + P_activation x (any SM active)

All pipes (FMA, TC, LSU) draw identical total power at nvidia-smi resolution (~60.7W) once at least one SM is active. The per-pipe differences (0.065-0.23 W/SM) are measurable only through careful per-SM incremental analysis. At the chip level, the fixed activation cost (clock tree, L2, memory controllers, I/O ring) dominates, and the per-SM compute power is a small fraction of total draw.

9.8 Methodology Notes

  • Power measured via nvidia-smi --query-gpu=power.draw --format=csv,noheader,nounits polled at 100ms intervals during sustained workloads.
  • Clocks pinned via nvidia-smi -lgc {min},{max} to eliminate DVFS variation during efficiency measurements; unlocked for DVFS sweep cases.
  • Throughput calculated from instruction counts x clock frequency x SM count x ops-per-instruction, cross-validated against nvidia-smi reported GPU utilization.
  • All sustained tests: 60s minimum (Cases 9, 15, 16), up to 621s (Case 10), with power sampling throughout.
  • Temperature readings from nvidia-smi --query-gpu=temperature.gpu; GPU fan on auto throughout.
  • Case 17 (Sparse INT8) is a retest — the original measurement showed anomalous 1:1 sparse/dense ratio caused by ptxas inserting 4 MOV instructions per IMMA.SP due to 50-register pressure; retest with reduced register pressure confirms native 2:1 sparse acceleration.
  • DRAM clock sweep (Case 13) via nvidia-smi -lmc reveals only 3 discrete operating points despite 5 target clocks, indicating GDDR7 operates at fixed frequency bins.
